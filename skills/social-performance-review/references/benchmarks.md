# SMB Social Media Benchmarks

Reference benchmarks for evaluating SMB performance. These are averages for organic (non-paid) content on business accounts. Performance varies significantly by industry, account size, and content type — use as a guide, not a verdict.

---

## Instagram (Business Accounts)

### Engagement Rate (by follower count)
Engagement rate = (likes + comments + saves + shares) ÷ reach × 100

| Followers | Below average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Under 1K | < 3% | 3-6% | 6-10% | 10%+ |
| 1K–10K | < 2% | 2-4% | 4-7% | 7%+ |
| 10K–50K | < 1.5% | 1.5-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| 50K+ | < 1% | 1-2% | 2-4% | 4%+ |

**Note:** Smaller accounts typically see higher engagement rates. Don't compare a 500-follower account to an industry report benchmarked on accounts with 50K+.

### Save Rate (saves ÷ reach × 100)
The most meaningful signal for content quality on Instagram.

| Rating | Save rate |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 0.5% |
| Average | 0.5-1.5% |
| Good | 1.5-3% |
| Excellent | 3%+ |

### Reach Rate (reach ÷ followers × 100)

| Rating | Reach rate |
|---|---|
| Declining (algorithm not serving content) | < 10% |
| Average | 10-20% |
| Good | 20-35% |
| Excellent (strong algorithm signal) | 35%+ |

**Note:** Reels are served to non-followers and can show 100%+ reach rate — exclude reels when benchmarking reach rate against followers.

### Follower Growth Rate (monthly)

| Rating | Monthly growth |
|---|---|
| Declining | Negative |
| Flat | 0-0.5% |
| Average | 0.5-2% |
| Good | 2-5% |
| Strong | 5%+ |

### Posting Frequency (recommended for SMBs)

| Account stage | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Starting out (under 500 followers) | 3-4x/week |
| Growing (500-5K followers) | 4-5x/week |
| Established (5K+ followers) | 4-6x/week |

## LinkedIn (Company Pages)

### Engagement Rate
Engagement rate = (reactions + comments + shares + clicks) ÷ impressions × 100

| Rating | Engagement rate |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 1% |
| Average | 1-3% |
| Good | 3-6% |
| Excellent | 6%+ |

**Note:** LinkedIn personal profiles typically see 2-3x higher engagement than company pages. If the client is posting from both, benchmark separately.

### Impressions per Post

Highly variable by industry and follower count. Use the client's own average as the baseline after 2-3 months.

### Follower Growth Rate (monthly)

| Rating | Monthly growth |
|---|---|
| Flat | 0-0.5% |
| Average | 0.5-2% |
| Good | 2-4% |
| Strong | 4%+ |

### Posting Frequency (recommended)

| Goal | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Brand awareness | 3-5x/week |
| Lead generation / thought leadership | 4-5x/week |

## Facebook (Business Pages)

Organic reach on Facebook has declined significantly since 2020. Facebook is primarily useful for local businesses with strong community engagement and for retargeting via paid.

### Organic Reach Rate (reach ÷ page likes × 100)

| Rating | Reach rate |
|---|---|
| Typical for most pages | 2-6% |
| Above average | 6-10% |
| Strong (usually community/group-adjacent) | 10%+ |

### Engagement Rate

| Rating | Engagement rate |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 0.5% |
| Average | 0.5-1% |
| Good | 1-2% |
| Excellent | 2%+ |

**Interpretation note:** Facebook's low engagement rate benchmarks reflect the platform's declining organic performance. A 1% rate on Facebook is solid. Do not compare to Instagram rates directly.

## TikTok (Business Accounts)

### Engagement Rate
(likes + comments + shares) ÷ views × 100

| Rating | Engagement rate |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 3% |
| Average | 3-6% |
| Good | 6-10% |
| Excellent | 10%+ |

### View Rate and Completion Rate

| Metric | Average | Good |
|---|---|---|
| % watched to completion (under 15s) | 20-40% | 40%+ |
| % watched to completion (15-30s) | 15-25% | 25%+ |

**Note:** TikTok distributes to non-followers by default. Views and reach data is not comparable to Instagram — a small account can get large view counts without a large following.

## Format Benchmarks (Instagram)

Average performance relative to single image posts (index: single image = 100):

| Format | Reach | Saves | Comments | Engagement rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Single image | 100 | 100 | 100 | 100 |
| Carousel | 90-110 | 200-300 | 120-150 | 130-180 |
| Reel (short video) | 200-400 | 80-120 | 90-130 | 100-150 |

**Interpretation:**
- Carousels: lower or similar reach to single images, but significantly higher saves. Best for educational and tips content.
- Reels: highest reach (discovery algorithm), but saves and comments are lower. Best for awareness and follower growth.
- Single images: reliable, consistent, lowest effort. Good for product showcases and social proof.

## Using These Benchmarks

1. **First review** — compare the client's metrics against these benchmarks to identify gaps
2. **Subsequent reviews** — the client's own prior month becomes the primary benchmark. External benchmarks become less important as you have real trend data
3. **Industry adjustment** — highly visual industries (food, beauty, fitness) typically see above-average Instagram engagement. B2B and professional services typically see below-average. Adjust expectations accordingly.
4. **Never present benchmarks as targets** — they are context, not goals. The goal is consistent improvement month-over-month.
